03 — What you've already tried

The honest part

You've already done the work. Here's why it didn't hold.

Most approaches to this work are designed to give you an experience. Threshold was designed to give you a protocol. But a protocol without a container is just information — and you have enough information. The Presence Collective is the container.

04 — Promise · who this is for

This program is for people who have already done the thinking.

The Presence Collective isn't an introduction. It's designed for people who have attended Threshold and now want a structured container for the work to continue — not a course to consume, but a practice to inhabit.

  • You want structure without rigidity. A weekly container that holds the work without prescribing the experience.

  • You've tried solo practice and hit a ceiling. The work deepens in community — this is the room for that.

  • You are not looking for transformation narratives. You want specific, repeatable protocols that work in an ordinary life.

  • You are ready to invest in a slow result. Eight weeks is enough time to change a habit; it is not enough time to change a person. We are honest about this.

06 — Session breakdown · week by week
The eight weeks

What happens in each session

Every week has a focus. Every session has a protocol. Nothing is filler.

  • Orienting the room and establishing the practice container
  • Protocol 1 in full: the attention anchoring sequence
  • Daily 10-minute structure for the week
  • Q&A and integration check-in
  • Common resistance patterns and how to work with them, not around them
  • Adjusting Protocol 1 for different life contexts
  • Live practice with the group, real-time feedback from Lena
  • Protocol 2 in full: distinguishing aligned no from fear-based no
  • How to use the protocol on low-stakes decisions to build fluency
  • Common failure modes and how to catch them early
  • Integration session: weaving Protocols 1 and 2 into a single daily structure
  • Extended Q&A — Lena works directly with participants on their specific sticking points
  • Mid-program check-in and recalibration
  • Protocol 3 in full: the 90-second reset sequence
  • When and how to deploy it — context-specific applications
  • Practice in pairs within the group setting
  • Honest session: most people hit a resistance peak around week 5–6. We work with it directly.
  • Identifying the difference between genuine friction and productive discomfort
  • Adjustments to your practice structure based on what the first five weeks revealed
  • For most participants, this is when the practice begins to feel like theirs
  • Deepening the three protocols in combination
  • Beginning to build your post-program structure
  • Final integration: your complete practice structure, mapped out
  • Where to take this next — resources, continuation options, further reading
  • Celebration and honest reflection on the eight weeks
  • Final Q&A with Lena

Your facilitator

Lena Hartwell

Lena has spent fifteen years developing and refining the three protocols that form the foundation of this work. She trained in contemplative practice under Tibetan and Zen lineages before departing from institutional Buddhism to develop a non-denominational, protocol-based approach designed for people with no background in meditation or spiritual practice.
